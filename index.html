The Black Bull

2022

Everything burns. FTX takes the cycle down with it and Solana trades at eight dollars. The obituaries write themselves, and the industry lines up to sign.

One account refuses. While everyone else quietly deletes their takes, he leaves every one of his up.

2023

SOL claws its way from eight toward three hundred and the refusal becomes legend. Then Bonk. Then Wif. The trenches crown him The Solana Guy.

Ansem. A Georgia Tech engineer who chose the trenches over the cubicle. No fund behind him, no backers. He earned the name the way his followers try to: one trade at a time, in public.

June 2026

The trenches are a graveyard. Volume has bled out for months and the obituaries are back, this time for memecoins themselves. On June 17 an anonymous deployer spends $6,300 on pump.fun, mints a token called The Black Bull, and sends 650 million of it to Ansem's wallet. Unasked. A dare dressed as a gift.

He could have sold. Every precedent in this market said sell. Instead he went at pump.fun's reward machine in public and typed the line that lit the fuse:

"Had to give the trenches a stimmy."

Then he made it real. Roughly $7,000,000 airdropped back to the community in 72 hours, paid from the creator fees flowing into his own wallet. One drop cleared a million dollars. Hundreds of wallets woke up holding money nobody asked them to earn. A thirty-five dollar drop ran past six thousand.

Holders tripled in days. Token launches across Solana hit an 80-day high. SOL itself caught a bid. A dead market found a pulse because one trader handed the money back instead of walking off with it.

There is no roadmap here and no promise of one. The token is one man's word, settled on-chain, checkable line by line. That is either nothing at all or the most honest asset in the casino, and the market is deciding in real time.

The stated goal is a million holders. The real one sits on the scoreboard above: the platform that farmed the trenches for years, flipped by their own bull.
